03
Your plan — three phases in order
Every plan is structured correctly by design. Do them in sequence — activation first, dynamic second, static last.
Activation
Fire the muscle before loading it. Band, dumbbell, or bodyweight exercises to prime the joint and establish the movement pattern.
Do this first · before training
Pre-Workout Dynamic
Movement-based stretches that increase range of motion and mobilise the joints without reducing strength.
Do this second · before training
Post-Workout Static
Long passive holds that lengthen the muscles, reduce soreness, and restore resting length after training.
Do this after training
04
Edit, save, and reuse
Every card can be replaced with an alternative, removed, or expanded for full instructions. Save named plans for your regular splits — Leg Day, Push Day, Full Body Sunday — and load them in one tap.
Leg Day Push Day Full Body Sunday
⚑ The FlexFlow rule
Never static stretch before training. Static holds reduce force output by up to 8% if held for more than 60 seconds before a session. FlexFlow enforces the correct order by design — activation and dynamic always come before static, every time.
